About Ralph Lauren Early life/Career

Lauren was born on October 14, 1939, in the Bronx, New York City, to Ashkenazi Jewish immigrants Frieda (Cutler) and Frank Lifshitz, an artist and house painter. At the age of 16, he an his brother George Poitras Lauren legally changed their second names from Lifshitz to Lauren, due to bullying at school. As Lauren got older he attended Baruch college, where he studied business, but after two years of going. He suddenly dropped out. By 1962 to 1964, Ralph served in the united states army. After being relased he instantly picked up a job working for the Rivertz as a salesman. Due to his hard work and determination, Lauren was able to convinced the company president to let him start his own line.

How did Polo come about

In 1967, Lauren, then a young tie salesman, began his journey by creating wide, flamboyant ties under the label "Polo." The name itself evoked a sense of sophistication, evoking the sport of polo and the aspirational lifestyle associated with it. Lauren's ties struck a chord with consumers who were seeking something different from the prevailing fashion trends of the time. Fast forward to 1972, Ralph Lauren came out with his first full line of menswear with the Polo brand name. These clothes was refined yet rugged aesthetics of the English aristocracy and the American West. This debut collection embodied Lauren's distinctive blend of traditional craftsmanship and contemporary elegance, resonating with an audience eager for timeless, quality clothing that reflected a sense of heritage and adventure.

Ralph Lauren Philanthropy

Back in 1987, Ralph Lauren underwent surgery to remove a brain tumor. He ended up making a full recovery. Since then Lauren has put alot of time and effort to address cancer. By 1989, Ralph co-founded the Nina Hyde Center for Breast Cancer Research in Washington D.C. Due to the memory of Nina Hyde, a fashion correspondednt. As well as In 2003, Lauren supported the establishment the Ralph Lauren Center for Cancer Care and Prevention in Harlem, Manhattan, New York City. The Center is a collaboration between Ralph Lauren, Memorial Sloan-Kettering, and North General Hospital in Harlem. Ultimately from 2014-2016 the Ralph Lauren Corporation partnered with the Royal Marsden, the largest and most comprehensive cancer center in Europe, to develop a world-class breast cancer research facility. They opened the Royal Marsden Ralph Lauren Center for Breast Cancer Research.
